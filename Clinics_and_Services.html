Services

  • Basics of Health Insurance: What It Is and Why It's Needed

    Health insurance is financial protection in case of illness or injury. It allows patients to receive medical care without incurring significant out-of-pocket costs. The main idea is that, for a moderate monthly fee, a client gains access to a wide range of healthcare services — from consultations to hospitalization.

  • Diagnostics and Lab Tests

    Most insurance plans include coverage for diagnostic procedures such as MRI, CT scans, ultrasounds, blood tests, and screenings. This helps detect illnesses at early stages and begin timely treatment.

  • Consultation Services

    One of the basic services in health insurance includes free or partially covered doctor consultations. This can be a general practitioner or specialists such as a cardiologist, dermatologist, or gastroenterologist. Some insurance companies also offer online consultations with doctors.

  • Hospitalization and Inpatient Care

    A health insurance policy often covers the cost of hospital stays, including surgeries, medications, meals, and even accommodation in a hospital ward. More advanced plans may allow patients to choose a private room or clinic.

  • Rehabilitation and Physiotherapy

    After surgeries or serious illnesses, recovery is often needed. Health insurance may cover physiotherapy sessions, massages, psychological support, or specialized rehabilitation programs in clinics.

  • Emergency Coverage Abroad

    Some insurance policies include travel medical insurance, covering emergency care during trips: hospital transportation, treatment costs, evacuation, and more. This is especially useful for students and frequent travelers.

  • Mental Health Coverage

    An increasing number of insurance companies include mental health support: psychological consultations, psychotherapy, online assistance, and hotlines. This is crucial in today’s world where stress and anxiety levels are rising.

  • Family Health Insurance

    Family policies allow coverage for several family members at once. It is more cost-effective and ensures children, parents, and spouses have comprehensive medical support.

  • Elderly Care

    For seniors, there are special insurance packages that cover routine checkups, chronic illnesses, home care, or care in specialized facilities. This enables older adults to receive quality care without significant expenses.

  • Preventive Programs

    Insurance may include annual health checkups, vaccinations, risk assessments, and healthy lifestyle programs — all designed to prevent diseases.

  • Additional Services: Dentistry, Ophthalmology, Alternative Medicine

    Some extended packages cover dental treatment, eye care, and alternative methods such as acupuncture, homeopathy, and massage therapy.
